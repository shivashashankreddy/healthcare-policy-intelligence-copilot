# Synthetic Medical Necessity Appeals Policy

This synthetic appeals policy describes sample evidence review steps for medical necessity appeals.

Appeals must be reviewed by staff who were not involved in the original adverse determination. The review record should include the original rationale, appeal narrative, supporting clinical documentation, applicable policy section, and final determination reason.

An appeal may overturn the original decision when new documentation resolves the specific reason for denial, when the original decision cited the wrong policy section, or when an exception pathway is clearly satisfied.

An appeal should be upheld when submitted evidence does not address the denial reason or when the policy criteria remain unmet. Ambiguous cases should be escalated for clinical or compliance review.
